## Notes

- **US-xGR, US-YK2, US-CRK**: still excluded from the 179-site RF benchmark (see `README_benchmark_exclusions.md`).
- **US-xAB (Yacolt Burn)**: The site name references the 1902 Yacolt fire (~238,000 acres), one of the largest fires in Washington State history, but the current flux tower footprint is second-growth industrial forest, not actively fire-disturbed during the measurement period.
- **US-Vcm**: The 2013 wildfire is particularly important — it caused a complete stand replacement and a long data gap; all pre-2013 flux observations reflect the undisturbed stand, all post-2014 observations reflect early post-fire recovery.
- **US-GLE**: The ~2008 spruce beetle outbreak caused ~85% overstory mortality; this is one of the clearest stand-replacing biotic disturbance examples in the dataset.
- **ZA-BfK, ZA-BfS**: Very limited online documentation. These appear to be newer FLUXNET sites not well-covered by English-language web resources. Recommend contacting NRF-SAEON or the site PI directly for disturbance records.
