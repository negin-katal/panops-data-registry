## Notes on Data Quality and Verification

- **Web access was restricted** during compilation. WebSearch was denied; most WebFetch calls were denied; fluxnet.org returned HTTP 403; AmeriFlux returned "No data" for non-US sites. All entries are based on training-data knowledge synthesized from peer-reviewed literature, FLUXNET site papers, and ICOS/AmeriFlux documentation available before August 2025.
- **Site names and IGBP classes** verified against local file: `/mnt/gsdata/projects/panops/panops-data-registry/data/flux/fluxnet_2017_2025_V02/fluxnet_site_metadata_clean.csv`.
- **Priority sites for manual verification:**
  - **ES-HeB** (site name explicitly says "Burnt" — verify exact fire year from site PI or ICOS ES)
  - **FR-Bil** (2022 Landes fires — verify if tower footprint directly burned)
  - **FI-Hyy** (stand age ~1962 clear-cut origin — well documented but verify thinning years)
  - **IL-Yat** (afforestation year ~1964 — verify from JNF records)
  - **IT-Ren** (2018–2019 South Tyrol bark beetle — verify if tower footprint affected)
  - **JP-Ynf** (typhoon disturbance — check for specific events)
- **Recommended verification steps:**
  1. Check ICOS site pages: `https://www.icos-cp.eu/observations/ecosystem/sites`
  2. Check live AmeriFlux pages: `https://ameriflux.lbl.gov/sites/siteinfo/SITE-ID` (note: non-US sites show "No data" in current database)
  3. Check site PI publications and FLUXNET site papers
  4. For FR-Bil: cross-reference with 2022 Gironde wildfire perimeter maps (French DFCI database)
  5. For ES-HeB/ES-Hen: contact CSIC/Univ. Murcia PIs for exact fire year
  6. For IT-Ren: check Senf & Seidl (2021) European disturbance map for South Tyrol 2018–2019
